Lowell's 3 1/2 methods for identifying minerals using EDS

  1. The Reed Book: If you look in the appendices at the end of "the Reed Book," you'll find many examples of EDS spectra collected from common minerals. Collect a point spectrum from your mystery mineral and try to match it with one of these example spectra.
  2. The DHZ Book: The mineralogy textbook(s) by Deer Howie and Zussman (DHZ) include many examples of bulk compositions of minerals. Collect a standardless EDS analysis (or WDS analysis) of your mystery mineral and try to match the composition with one of these compositions.
  3. Calculate a mineral formula: Once you have used one or both of the two methods above to obtain a "candidate mineral" that your mystery mineral could be, look up the mineral formula to find the number of O atoms, and calculate the number of cations from your oxide composition. This doesn't apply to minerals without O (pyrite, etc).

...or you can just query Google or ChatGPT with a question of the form "what is a mineral containing elements A, B, and C that commonly occurs in rock type X?" Sometimes you'll get lucky and the answer won't be too inaccurate.
